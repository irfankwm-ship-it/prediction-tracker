How We Categorize Conflict Trajectories

Each conflict is assigned a trajectory based on observable changes in the past 30 days. "Stable" does not mean "peaceful" - it means the conflict continues at a consistent level without significant escalation or de-escalation. Our analysts review casualty data, territorial changes, diplomatic activity, and weapons deployments weekly.

ESCALATING

Active increase in hostilities, casualties, or geographic scope within the past 30 days.

Key Indicators:
  • Rising casualty counts week-over-week
  • New weapons systems introduced
  • Geographic expansion of fighting
  • Breakdown of ceasefire or negotiations
  • Third-party intervention or threatened intervention

STABLE

Conflict continues at consistent intensity without significant escalation or de-escalation.

Key Indicators:
  • Frontlines largely static
  • Casualty rates within historical range
  • No major territorial changes
  • Diplomatic channels open but not progressing
  • Humanitarian situation persistent but not worsening

DE-ESCALATING

Active reduction in hostilities, often accompanied by diplomatic progress.

Key Indicators:
  • Decreasing casualty counts
  • Ceasefire holding or expanding
  • Prisoner exchanges or humanitarian corridors
  • Third-party mediation advancing
  • Troop withdrawals or repositioning

Ukraine-Russia War

Eastern Europe
ESCALATING
Start: February 2022 Casualties: 500,000+ (combined, estimated) Displaced: 6.5 million refugees, 5 million internally displaced
Parties: Russia, Ukraine, NATO (support)

Summary

Full-scale Russian invasion of Ukraine following 2014 Crimea annexation. Largest European conflict since WWII.

Current Situation

Frontlines largely static in Donbas region. Russia consolidating gains in Luhansk and Donetsk oblasts. Ukraine conducting long-range strikes into Russian territory using Western-supplied weapons.

Recent Developments

  • Russia deploying hypersonic missiles (Kinzhal, Zircon) against Ukrainian infrastructure
  • Ukraine targeting Russian oil refineries and military bases with drone swarms
  • North Korean troops reportedly deployed to rear areas
  • F-16 deliveries beginning from European allies

Why "ESCALATING"?

Classified as ESCALATING due to: introduction of new weapons systems (hypersonic missiles, Western F-16s), geographic expansion of strikes (both sides now hitting deep targets), and breakdown of peace negotiations following Swiss summit.

Key Battlefronts

Bakhmut axis, Zaporizhzhia front, Kharkiv oblast border

Outlook

High-intensity conflict likely to continue through 2026. Neither side shows willingness to negotiate. Risk of escalation if NATO directly involved or if Russia uses tactical nuclear weapons.

Gaza Conflict

Middle East
STABLE
Start: October 2023 Casualties: 40,000+ Palestinian, 1,400+ Israeli (as of late 2025) Displaced: 1.9 million (90% of Gaza population)
Parties: Israel, Hamas, Palestinian Islamic Jihad, Hezbollah (related)

Summary

Israeli military operation in Gaza following October 7, 2023 Hamas attack that killed 1,200+ Israelis.

Current Situation

Israeli ground forces control northern Gaza and Rafah border. Hamas maintains guerrilla resistance. Humanitarian catastrophe ongoing with severe food and medical shortages.

Recent Developments

  • Ceasefire negotiations continue through Qatar and Egypt mediation
  • Hostage releases occurring in limited exchanges
  • ICC arrest warrants issued for Israeli and Hamas leaders
  • Houthi attacks on Red Sea shipping continue in solidarity

Why "STABLE"?

Classified as STABLE (not de-escalating) because: combat operations continue at reduced intensity, no comprehensive ceasefire achieved, hostages remain in captivity, and humanitarian crisis shows no improvement. 'Stable' here means consistent conflict level, not peace.

Key Battlefronts

Khan Younis, Rafah crossing, Northern Gaza tunnels

Outlook

Conflict likely to remain at current intensity. Political resolution requires Palestinian governance plan that no party has proposed. Regional spillover risk remains elevated.

Sudan Civil War

Africa
ESCALATING
Start: April 2023 Casualties: 15,000+ killed Displaced: 10 million internally, 2+ million refugees
Parties: Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF), Rapid Support Forces (RSF)

Summary

Power struggle between SAF (Gen. Burhan) and RSF (Gen. Hemedti) following failed democratic transition.

Current Situation

RSF controls most of Darfur and parts of Khartoum. SAF holds Port Sudan and eastern regions. Ethnic violence in Darfur raising genocide concerns.

Recent Developments

  • RSF advances in Sennar and Blue Nile states
  • UAE accused of supplying weapons to RSF via Chad
  • UN warns of 'world's largest displacement crisis'
  • Egyptian military intervention increasingly likely

Why "ESCALATING"?

Classified as ESCALATING due to: RSF territorial gains in new states, ethnic cleansing reports from Darfur, external power involvement (UAE, Egypt, Ethiopia), and complete collapse of humanitarian access.

Key Battlefronts

Khartoum, El Fasher (Darfur), Sennar state

Outlook

Trajectory toward state collapse. No credible peace process. Regional destabilization affecting Chad, South Sudan, Ethiopia, Egypt.

  • UN-brokered truce largely holding domestically

Key Battlefronts

Marib front (quiet), Hodeidah (truce), Red Sea (Houthi operations)

Outlook

Domestic conflict may be winding down. Red Sea operations could invite Western intervention. Saudi seeking exit strategy.

Outlook

Junta facing existential threat. 2025-2026 may see accelerated collapse. Post-junta governance uncertain with multiple armed groups.

Syria Civil War

Middle East
STABLE
Start: March 2011 Casualties: 500,000+ killed Displaced: 6.8 million refugees, 6.9 million internally displaced
Parties: Assad Government, Various opposition groups, SDF/Kurds, Turkey, Russia, Iran, Israel

Summary

Multi-sided conflict evolved from 2011 uprising. Assad regime recaptured most territory with Russian/Iranian support.

Current Situation

Government controls ~70% of territory. Idlib remains opposition holdout. Northeast under Kurdish SDF control with US presence. Turkey maintains buffer zone in north.

Recent Developments

  • Israeli strikes on Iranian positions continue weekly
  • Normalization with Arab states progressing
  • Turkish operations against PKK/SDF ongoing
  • Reconstruction beginning in government areas

Why "STABLE"?

Classified as STABLE because: no major territorial changes since 2020, conflict frozen in current configuration, all parties maintaining positions without offensive operations. Low-intensity violence continues but not escalating.

Key Battlefronts

Idlib (frozen), Al-Tanf (US base), Northeast (SDF)

Outlook

Frozen conflict likely to persist. Assad regime rehabilitated regionally. Kurdish autonomy question unresolved. Reconstruction dependent on sanctions relief.
