About Conflict Monitoring: We track active conflicts worldwide using ACLED data, news analysis, and satellite imagery. Each conflict is assessed for trajectory (escalating, stable, or de-escalating) based on recent casualty trends, territorial changes, and diplomatic developments.

Active Conflicts

Ukraine-Russia War ESCALATING

Russia deployed its new Oreshnik hypersonic missile in a major attack on Ukraine, marking a significant escalation beyond conventional warfare tactics. The weapon's deployment signals Russia's willingness to use advanced strategic weapons. NATO allies are increasing military aid in response, while diplomatic channels remain frozen.

Region: Eastern Europe Started: February 2022 Casualties: 500,000+
Gaza Conflict STABLE

Ceasefire negotiations continue with sporadic violations from both sides. The humanitarian situation remains critical with severely restricted aid access to civilian populations. International pressure mounts for a lasting peace agreement, with Qatar and Egypt leading mediation efforts.

Region: Middle East Escalated: October 2023 Displaced: 1.9M
Sudan Civil War ESCALATING

Fighting between the Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) and Rapid Support Forces (RSF) intensifies in Darfur and Khartoum. Over 10 million people have been displaced, creating Africa's largest displacement crisis. Regional spillover threatens stability in Chad and South Sudan.

Region: East Africa Started: April 2023 Displaced: 10M+
Myanmar Civil War ESCALATING

Resistance forces continue to gain ground against the military junta across multiple regions. Ethnic armed organizations are increasingly coordinating with the People's Defense Force. China has increased border security as refugee flows grow, while the regime faces mounting economic pressure.

Region: Southeast Asia Escalated: February 2021 Displaced: 2.6M
Haiti Gang Violence ESCALATING

Gang coalitions now control approximately 80% of Port-au-Prince. The Kenyan-led multinational security mission faces severe resource shortages and limited operational capacity. Food insecurity has reached famine levels in urban areas, with humanitarian access increasingly restricted.

Region: Caribbean Escalated: 2024 Affected: 5.5M
Yemen Civil War STABLE

The UN-brokered truce largely holds despite Houthi attacks on Red Sea shipping. Saudi-Houthi backchannel negotiations continue with cautious progress. Humanitarian access has improved but 21 million Yemenis still require assistance, with famine risks persisting in remote areas.

Region: Middle East Started: 2014 Need Aid: 21M
